Here's what the data say about EMS response times in New York City

Both response times and the number of emergency calls made increased every year between 2020 and 2023, according to data from the City of New York.

By Camille Baker

It took longer, on average, for first responders to reach the scene of an emergency in 2023 than it had in the three years prior, according to data from the city of New York.

The data, which are publicly available, show that both the number of emergency calls made and response times increased every year since 2020.

In 2020, during the early stages of the pandemic, over 1.4 calls to emergency medical services were recorded. This same year, the mean response time of first responders - the time between when an emergency occurred and when responders arrived on the scene - was just over seven minutes. The mean response time, according to my analysis, was greater, about nine minutes. (This discrepancy is likely because emergency services intentionally respond to the most serious emergencies more quickly.)

The year following, 2021, the number of emergency medical services calls increased by about 80,000. However, according to my analysis, the median response time for EMS calls across all five boroughs and over the course of the year increased by only about five seconds to just over seven minutes. The mean response time this in 2021 fell slightly when compared with the year previous.

Though the seven-day average of Covid hospitalizations (though not deaths or cases) in New York City never again reached the level it had in early 2020, according to data from the New York City Department of Health, the number of EMS calls continued to rise in 2022 by a similar amount, topping over 1.5 million calls recorded in 2022. The median response time to all calls rose accordingly, by nearly a minute, and the mean response time rose by over a minute.

In 2023, the number of emergency calls recorded and their average response times rose again, but by a much smaller margin. Only about 2,000 more emergency calls were recorded in 2023 than were in the year prior.
